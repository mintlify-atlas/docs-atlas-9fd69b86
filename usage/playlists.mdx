title: "Playlists"
description: "Browse YouTube playlists indexed from your downloads, subscribe to playlists, and create your own custom playlists."
---

Tube Archivist supports two types of playlists: **YouTube playlists** that are automatically indexed when you download videos, and **custom playlists** that you create yourself inside Tube Archivist.

## YouTube playlists

YouTube playlists are indexed automatically. When you download a video that belongs to a YouTube playlist, Tube Archivist fetches the playlist metadata and adds it to the index. You do not need to do anything manually — the playlist appears on the Playlists page as soon as a video from it is downloaded.

Each YouTube playlist record stores:

- Playlist name and description
- The owning channel
- An ordered list of all entries in the playlist, including which ones you have downloaded
- A thumbnail (taken from the first downloaded video in the playlist)

<Note>
  If a video belongs to multiple playlists, it appears in all of them. Playlist membership is stored as metadata on the video itself.
</Note>

### Subscribing to a playlist

Subscribing to a YouTube playlist means Tube Archivist will check for new videos in that playlist during each rescan, just as it does for channel subscriptions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the add form">
    Click the **+** (add) icon on the Playlists page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter playlist IDs or URLs">
    Paste one or more YouTube playlist URLs or IDs into the **Subscribe to playlists** text area:

    ```text
    https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLxxxxxx
    PLxxxxxx
    ```

    You can enter multiple playlists, one per line.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Subscribe">
    Tube Archivist starts a background task to index the playlist and fetch its metadata. A notification shows progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Unsubscribing from a playlist

Open the playlist detail page and toggle the **Subscribed** switch to off, or use the unsubscribe button on the playlist card. The playlist and its metadata remain in your index — new videos will no longer be queued automatically.

### Deleting a playlist

On the playlist detail page, click **Delete playlist**. You can choose to delete only the playlist metadata (leaving downloaded videos untouched) or delete the playlist together with all its downloaded video files.

<Warning>
  Deleting a playlist with its videos permanently removes the media files from disk and from the Elasticsearch index. This cannot be undone.
</Warning>

## Custom playlists

Custom playlists are playlists you create entirely within Tube Archivist. They are not tied to any YouTube playlist ID and can contain any downloaded videos from your archive.

### Creating a custom playlist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the add form">
    Click the **+** (add) icon on the Playlists page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a name">
    In the **Or create custom playlist** section, type a name for your playlist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create">
    The playlist is created immediately and appears on the Playlists page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Adding videos to a custom playlist

Open the custom playlist detail page and use the add video control to search for and add videos from your archive. You can also add a video to a custom playlist directly from the video detail page.

### Reordering and removing videos

On the custom playlist detail page, each video entry has controls to move it:

| Action | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Up** | Move the video one position earlier in the playlist |
| **Down** | Move the video one position later in the playlist |
| **Top** | Move the video to the first position |
| **Bottom** | Move the video to the last position |
| **Remove** | Remove the video from the playlist (the video itself is not deleted) |

<Note>
  When **Hide watched** is enabled in your user settings, moving a video up or down skips over already-watched entries so the video lands in a position next to an unwatched video.
</Note>

## Browsing the Playlists page

The Playlists page shows all indexed playlists — both YouTube playlists and custom playlists. Each card displays the playlist thumbnail, name, owning channel (for YouTube playlists), and entry count.

### View styles

Use the grid and list icons in the top-right toolbar to switch between grid view and list view.

### Filtering by subscription state

Click the filter icon to show only subscribed playlists or only unsubscribed playlists.

## Playlist detail page

Click any playlist to open its detail page. The page lists all videos in the playlist in their defined order.

- Downloaded videos are shown as playable cards.
- Entries that have not been downloaded yet are shown in a dimmed state.
- The **Play all** button starts playback from the first downloaded video and uses the playlist order for navigation.

### Sort order

For YouTube playlists you can set the sort order — **top** (original YouTube order) or **bottom** (reversed). This controls the order in which videos are fetched from YouTube during updates and how they are displayed on the playlist detail page.

## Differences between YouTube and custom playlists

| Feature | YouTube playlist | Custom playlist |
|---|---|---|
| Source | Indexed from YouTube | Created in Tube Archivist |
| Auto-updates | Yes, when subscribed | No |
| Channel association | Yes | No |
| Can reorder videos | No (follows YouTube order) | Yes |
| Can be subscribed | Yes | No |
| Thumbnail | From playlist on YouTube | From first downloaded video |
