---
title: "Getting started"
description: "Open the web interface, configure download settings, subscribe to channels, and start building your archive."
---

After the stack is running and the health check passes, open `http://your-host:8000` in your browser. Follow the steps below to complete the initial setup and start your first download.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in">
    Navigate to `http://your-host:8000`. You will be redirected to the login page.

    Enter the `TA_USERNAME` and `TA_PASSWORD` values you set in your `docker-compose.yml`, then click **Log in**.

    <Tip>
      The current version number is displayed in the footer. Check it after any update to confirm you are running the expected release.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure download format">
    Go to **Settings** and locate the **Download Format** field under the Download section.

    By default, Tube Archivist downloads the best available quality. If you plan to play videos in Safari, on iOS, or on macOS, set a compatible format explicitly:

    ```
    bestvideo[vcodec*=avc1]+bestaudio[acodec*=mp4a]/mp4
    ```

    This selects H.264 video with AAC audio — the format with the broadest browser and device compatibility.

    <Note>
      Not every codec is playable in every browser. Test your format selection with a small download before archiving an entire channel.
    </Note>

    Save your settings before continuing.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Subscribe to channels">
    Go to the **Channels** page and search for the YouTube channels you want to archive.

    Click **Subscribe** on any channel to add it to your subscription list. Tube Archivist will use your subscriptions to populate the download queue when you rescan.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add videos to the download queue">
    Go to the **Downloads** page. You have two options:

    - **Rescan subscriptions** — Queries YouTube for new uploads from all subscribed channels and adds them to the queue. Use this to sync your queue with the latest uploads.
    - **Add to download queue** — Manually add one or more items by pasting YouTube video URLs, channel URLs, or playlist URLs. You can add multiple items at once.

    <Tip>
      Use **Add to download queue** to download a specific video or backfill an entire channel or playlist without subscribing to it.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start downloading">
    Click **Start download** on the Downloads page. Tube Archivist begins downloading the queued items using `yt-dlp`, saves the video files to the `media` volume, and indexes the metadata in Elasticsearch.

    You can monitor progress on the Downloads page. The queue updates in real time as items complete.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Browse and search your archive">
    Once downloads complete, your videos appear on the **Home** page.

    - Use the **search bar** at the top to run full-text searches across video titles, descriptions, subtitles, and comments.
    - Browse by channel or playlist using the sidebar navigation.
    - Mark videos as watched to keep track of what you have seen.

    <Tip>
      Set up scheduled tasks in **Settings → Scheduling** to automatically rescan subscriptions and start downloads on a recurring schedule — no manual intervention required.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
